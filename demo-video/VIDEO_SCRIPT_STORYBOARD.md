## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Equipment Needed
- Samsung Galaxy Fold 7 (working demo installation)
- Tripod with phone mount (top-down shots)
- LED ring light (eliminate screen glare)
- Screen recording software (Android): AZ Screen Recorder, Mobizen
- Video editing: DaVinci Resolve (free), Adobe Premiere
- Voiceover: Professional (Fiverr: $50-100) or text-only version

### Shooting Tips
1. **Clean the screens**: Fingerprints show in close-ups
2. **Reduce brightness**: 70% to avoid blown-out highlights
3. **120Hz recording**: Use 60fps camera to capture smooth animations
4. **Stabilization**: Use tripod for all shots (no handheld)
5. **B-Roll**: Shoot 3x more footage than needed for editing flexibility

### Screen Recording Settings
- **Resolution**: 1440p (native Fold 7 resolution)
- **Frame Rate**: 60 FPS (half of 120Hz for compatibility)
- **Bitrate**: 50 Mbps (high quality)
- **Audio**: Record system audio + narration separately

### Editing Workflow
1. **Rough Cut**: Assemble all clips in timeline (no effects)
2. **Timing**: Sync narration/text to visuals (2:00 target)
3. **Color Grade**: Enhance screen visibility (boost saturation slightly)
4. **Sound Mix**: Music at -24dB, narration at -6dB, SFX at -18dB
5. **Export**: H.264, 1080p60, 10 Mbps for YouTube

### Music Recommendations (Royalty-Free)
- **Epidemic Sound**: "Digital Horizons" by Cerclo
- **Artlist**: "Quantum Dreams" by Artificial Music
- **YouTube Audio Library**: "Ambient Technology" genre

### Publishing
- **YouTube**: Upload as unlisted first, test on mobile devices
- **Title**: "Quantum Consciousness on Samsung Fold 7 | DNA-Lang Demo"
- **Description**: Include GitHub link, technical specs, call-to-action
- **Tags**: quantum computing, samsung fold 7, android, linux kernel, mobile quantum
- **Thumbnail**: Fold 7 with glowing Bloch sphere (1280x720, bright colors)
